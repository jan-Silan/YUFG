Youth United for Good

Who are we?

We are a youth-led, non-profit advocacy group which raises money for the most cost-effective, efficient charities in the world.
We arrange non-uniform days, bake sales, and quizzes at schools and universities across the world to raise money for various charitable causes. Our network includes over 70 members, including more than 50 schools and two universities, LSE and Oxford. With a far-reaching presence, our student network spans three countries: the UK, UAE, and the USA. With the support of our student representatives, we have already raised over £3,000.
At Youth United for Good, we are committed to supporting organisations that have a proven track record of saving lives and improving health outcomes efficiently. Our current fundraising efforts are focused on two of the most cost-effective charities in the world: The Malaria Consortium and Doctors without Borders (Médecins Sans Frontières).
By focusing on these high-impact charities, Youth United for Good ensures that your donations have the power to save lives and make lasting change where it matters most.
Youth United for Good also runs school assemblies and workshops on effective altruism and provides advice and consulting for young people who want to undertake ambitious social action projects both in their local areas and with our partner charities.

Our Mission

At Youth United for Good, our mission is both simple and impactful: to unite young people in raising funds for the world’s most cost-effective charities whilst creating real and measurable solutions to global challenges.
We believe in the power of youth to change the world. By working together, we can amplify our efforts to combat diseases, improve healthcare, and save lives in some of the most vulnerable communities worldwide.
Every donation we raise supports carefully selected charities that are proven to deliver maximum impact with every contribution. This ensures that our collective efforts bring the greatest good to the greatest number of people.
